# SooGo Korean Copy Guidelines

## Purpose

SooGo is a Korean-first outbound travel recommendation product. Write every user-facing string for Korean travelers departing from Korea, not for a global English audience.

## Product Voice

- Clear and grounded: say what the product helps with in one read.
- Warm but not overly cute: friendly service tone, not slang-heavy or playful for its own sake.
- Confident and practical: explain why a destination fits, what to check, and what the next action is.
- Recommendation-first: Instagram-style evidence is a supporting mood layer, never the ranking authority.
- Anonymous-first: remind users that they can use, save, and compare without login when that context matters.

## Core Tone Rules

- Lead with Korean. Keep English only for brand names, airport codes, official source names, or destination names when they add clarity.
- Prefer natural Korean product phrasing over literal translation.
- Use concise sentences. Aim for one idea per sentence.
- Speak directly to the traveler with everyday service language such as `-해요`, `-해 보세요`, `-할 수 있어요`.
- Avoid stiff admin wording such as `조회되었습니다`, `처리 중입니다`, `불가능합니다` unless the context is legal or technical.

## UI Writing Rules

- Buttons: action-first and short. Examples: `추천 받기`, `공유 페이지 보기`, `링크 복사`.
- Section titles: noun-based and scannable. Examples: `추천 이유`, `체크할 점`, `여행 정보`.
- Helper text: explain why the choice matters, not just what the control is.
- Empty states: explain the reason and suggest the next relaxation step.
- Error states: state what failed, keep the tone calm, and give one clear recovery action.
- Comparison and restore pages: emphasize that saved results are immutable and anonymous.

## Travel Context Rules

- Assume the traveler is departing from Korea.
- Reference Korean travel habits naturally: short annual leave, seasonal demand, airport choice, flight fatigue, value for money.
- Describe destinations in terms Korean travelers care about: travel window, budget feel, pace, flight distance, food, shopping, scenery, and atmosphere.
- Treat Instagram as mood proof only. Do not imply that social buzz decides the ranking.

## Terminology

- Use `목적지` over awkward literal translations like `데스티네이션`.
- Use `분위기` or `무드` sparingly; default to `분위기`.
- Use `추천 결과`, `비교 보드`, `저장한 카드` consistently.
- Use `예산 감각`, `비행 거리`, `추천 시기`, `일정 밀도` for recurring labels.

## Do Not

- Do not write English-first headlines with Korean only as a subtitle.
- Do not use machine-like direct translation from the previous English copy.
- Do not overuse exclamation marks, trendy slang, or influencer-style hype.
- Do not promise booking, pricing certainty, or real-time availability the product does not provide.
- Do not frame Instagram evidence as the main scoring engine.

## Example Pattern

- Bad: `Run recommendation`
- Good: `추천 받기`

- Bad: `The recommendation engine could not load a route right now.`
- Good: `지금은 추천 결과를 불러오지 못했어요. 잠시 후 다시 시도해 주세요.`

- Bad: `Instagram vibe summary`
- Good: `인스타그램 분위기 요약`
